Underwater Image Enhancement

This paper has been published in IJIRT Journal. Underwater imaging faces challenges like light scattering, color distortion, and low illumination, degrading image quality and limiting their use in fields such as marine biology and archaeology. This paper presents histogram-based techniques for enhancing underwater images, focusing on adaptive histogram equalization (AHE) and linear histogram manipulation to improve contrast, visibility, and detail extraction. These methods adjust pixel intensities to reveal hidden information, making them effective for processing large datasets and overcoming underwater imaging limitations.

Breast Cancer Detection and Analysis

The analysis of the Haberman's Survival Dataset indicates that the number of positive axillary nodes is a crucial predictor of breast cancer survival, with fewer nodes linked to longer survival. Patients who survived more than 5 years had an average of 2.98 nodes, while those who died within 5 years had 7.5 nodes on average. Age had a minimal impact on survival. The dataset, consisting of 289 observations, revealed that axillary node count is a key factor in predicting patient outcomes post-surgery.

Performance Analysis for Hybrid Beamforming using Deep Learning

Hybrid beamforming combines analog and digital techniques to optimize MIMO systems, but traditionally relies on complex channel state information (CSI). This paper proposes an unsupervised deep learning model that uses received signal strength indicator (RSSI) measurements instead of CSI, simplifying design and reducing costs. The approach improves spectral efficiency in FDD systems and avoids detailed CSI feedback. A deep neural network (DNN) is used for optimizing both beamforming components, leveraging a custom dataset for enhanced efficiency and robustness.
